Pearls are organic, which means they remember. They remember the perfume you sprayed near them, the sweat from a warm afternoon, the hairspray that drifted past. None of these ruin a pearl on first contact, but over years the cumulative exposure is what dulls the luster. A simple routine keeps them the way you bought them.

## Last on, first off

Put your pearls on after perfume, hairspray, and lotion. Take them off before your shower, your workout, and any chemical cleaning. The rule works because pearls can recover from a single exposure but not from fifty.

## Wipe after wearing

A soft microfiber cloth — the kind glasses come with — is enough. Wipe the pearls where they've touched skin, return them to their box. This takes ten seconds and extends the life of the strand by years.

## Store flat, store dry

Pearls don't like hanging on a hook; gravity stretches the silk thread over time. They also don't like airtight plastic, which traps moisture and can, eventually, make the nacre crack. A soft pouch in a dresser drawer is perfect.

## Restringing

Cultured pearl strands should be restrung every three to five years of regular wear, or immediately if you hear any shifting. Silk thread weakens slowly, then fails fast; restringing is cheap insurance. We offer it complimentary at the atelier on any strand we sold — and for a small fee on strands you bring in from elsewhere.

## The only thing that actually ruins them

Ultrasonic cleaners. The vibration loosens the nacre layers. Never use one on pearls, even briefly. Warm water and mild soap on a soft cloth is all they need.
